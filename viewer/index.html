The full picture

This simulates a university tutoring center across a whole semester. Every week, the planner builds a fresh weekly schedule (Step 1: match, Step 2: improve). Between weeks, things change — some students drop, some tutors get sick, some students don't show up, sometimes new requests arrive.

Navigating

Use the week tabs at the top to jump between weeks. Each tab shows that week's schedule, waiting list, and reasons.

The two colors of stripes

Diagonal red stripes on a tutor's row = that tutor is sick this week. Diagonal gray stripes in a slot = the room is booked by another department at that time.

The ★ on a student bubble

Means this student is with the same tutor as last week for the same topic — continuity bonus.

The yellow outline on a slot

Means it's overbooked — capacity +1 because the students likely to want it have a high no-show rate, so the planner sells more seats than physical capacity in expectation. If everyone shows up, one student gets bumped.

All 20 hard rules

1. Topic qualificationTutor must be qualified for the topic.
2. Pool routingSession pool (UG / Grad / Paid hourly) must match the student's pool.
3. LanguageSession's language must serve the student's preferred language.
4. Online vs in-personSession format must match the student's preference.
5. Wheelchair accessStudents needing access only in accessible rooms.
6. CaptioningStudents needing captioning only in captioning-capable online rooms.
7. Extra timeExtra-time students get 75-min sessions; the slot must accommodate that.
8. Group size1-on-1 students never put in a group slot.
9. Tutor weekly cap10h undergrad, 20h graduate, paid hourly capped by budget.
10. Tutor daily capNo tutor works more than 6h in any single day.
11. Tutor availabilityTutors only work in their declared weekly windows.
12. Back-to-back break15-minute break between consecutive sessions for the same tutor.
13. Travel timeTutors need extra time to move between buildings.
14. Class conflictsStudents can't be in a session during their own classes.
15. No double-bookingStudents or tutors can't be in two places at once.
16. Room exclusivitySame room can't host two sessions at the same time.
17. External room blocksRooms are sometimes booked by other departments — those times are off-limits.
18. EquipmentSome topics need a lab; the room must have it.
19. Zoom license capFixed number of online platforms; no more than that many online sessions at once.
20. Budget on paid hoursTotal paid hourly tutor hours ≤ the semester budget.
21. Preference-onlyStudent must have listed the session.

Soft goals (the quality score)

  • Place as many students as possible, especially priority ones, in their top preferences
  • Continuity across weeks (same tutor for same topic)
  • Anti-favoritism (no one tutor monopolizing one student)
  • Curricular order (prerequisite topics first)
  • Tutor load balance
  • Tutor travel time
  • Student wait time (gap between submission and session)

Dynamic events

  • Drops: ~5% of students permanently drop their course in weeks 1–2 (the add/drop period).
  • Tutor sickness: a random tutor or two falls ill each week; their schedule for that week is cancelled.
  • No-shows: students have a per-person no-show probability; the planner preemptively overbooks high-no-show slots.
  • New requests: requests can arrive after the weekly schedule is built — the planner places them on the fly.
  • Cancellations: a freed seat is offered to the highest-priority waiter.
